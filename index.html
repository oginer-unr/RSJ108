How To Write a Great Tweet

Time: 10 minutes
Rating: 85% Eye-catching Tweets

Twitter can be hard... but it doesn't have to be. Whether you're brand-new to Twitter or just made an account for business, use this guide to avoid cringe tweets.

  • Things You'll Need

  • A Twitter account
  • Something to tweet about
  • Access to Twitter on mobile or desktop
  • The bravery to hit send

  1. Think of your audience.

    Who are you tweeting to, and what about? Get yourself in the right headspace before you draft. A tweet about daily life may look a lot different than a tweet about the breaking news article you just wrote.

  2. Think of your tagsâ€”and don't use too many.

    Tags, hashtags, and links are a great way to engage your audience. The recommended number of tags in any tweet is two to three. If your tweet includes a company or person, make sure to tage them. If you're talking about a specific website, link it.

    When it comes to tags, remember the golden rule: don't force it. Out-of-place hashtags can be cringey and corporate to the average user.

  3. Get drafting!

    You have 280 characters to draft your perfect tweet, but remember that you don't have to use them all. For long links, using a URL shortener like bit.ly can make your posts more visually appealing on someone's timeline. Get your point across!

  4. Include a visual.

    Gifs and pictures help your audience visualize and relate to what you're talking about... even if it's just a reaction gif! If you're posting a photo, keep your dimensions in mind (you don't want any awkward crops, especially of people). Twitter's current photo dimensions are 1200px x 675px.

    To add extra personal flare, consider making your own gif from videos or live photos you've taken.

  5. Reread and hit send.

    After you've read over your tweet for any egregious typos, go and hit send! You've got this.
